What is Exercise Counseling?

Exercise Counseling is an opportunity for you to meet with one of our cancer exercise specialists who will provide you with a custom-designed exercise program. This service helps to establish the foundation of your individual exercise program as you cope and recover from cancer. Some of the issues that we consider in each person's exercise plan include recommendations for weight loss or gain, fatigue issues, range of motion concerns, and bone density. In an Exercise Counseling session discussions include health history, cancer diagnosis and treatments, current health concerns, exercise background, goals, resources and more.

What happens after my Exercise Counseling session?

For most survivors this means beginning a routine of regular walking, aerobic exercise, weight (or resistance) training, yoga, Pilates, swimming, or rowing. Your program might consist of individual and/or class exercise at a gym or health club or involve exercise in the privacy of your home, even movement in bed! We also try to contact you about two or three weeks after your Exercise Counseling session to offer encouragement, see if you've been able to start or continue exercising.
